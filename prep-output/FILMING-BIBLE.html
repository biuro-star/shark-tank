Wardrobe & Appearance

Vadym — What to Wear

  • Dark fitted T-shirt OR dark polo (navy, charcoal, or black)
  • Dark jeans or chinos — clean, fitted, no holes
  • Clean minimal sneakers (Nike, Adidas, or plain white/dark)
  • No jewelry except a simple wedding band (if worn daily)
  • Belt — simple, dark, no large buckle

What to Avoid

  • White shirt — washes out on camera
  • Busy patterns or stripes — distracting on TV
  • Visible BenchK logo on clothing — looks like an ad
  • Watch or bracelet — catches light, reflects into camera
  • Large logos of any brand
  • Shorts — must be standing, full-length pants

Grooming & Posture

Grooming Checklist

  • Fresh haircut (get it by Saturday May 2, not day-of)
  • Beard trimmed and shaped — clean neckline
  • Moisturize face morning-of — avoid shine (matte moisturizer or translucent powder)
  • Clean fingernails — hands will be on camera during demo
  • No sunglasses on head or tucked into shirt
  • Phone OFF and out of pockets (no visible bulge)

On-Camera Posture

  • Stand tall — shoulders back, chin level (not up, not down)
  • Feet shoulder-width apart — grounded, stable
  • Hands at sides when not gesturing — never in pockets
  • Lean slightly forward on power lines — shows conviction
  • No rocking, swaying, or shifting weight foot-to-foot
  • No coffee cup, water bottle, or props on set except BenchK wall

Night Before (Sunday May 3)

  • No alcohol
  • Light dinner — no heavy/greasy food
  • Sleep 8 hours — alarm at 6:30 AM
  • Lay out full outfit, belt, shoes
  • Charge phone to 100%

Morning Of (Monday May 4)

  • No coffee (can cause jitters and dry mouth on camera)
  • Light breakfast only
  • Hydrate well — stop drinking water 2 hours before filming to avoid bathroom breaks
  • 15-minute voice warm-up (see Energy & Performance Guide)
  • No phone calls before filming — preserve vocal energy

Voice Warm-Up (15 Minutes — Monday Morning)

Minutes 0–3: Breathing

  • Stand tall. Hand on belly, hand on chest.
  • Breathe in 4 sec (belly out). Hold 2 sec. Exhale 6 sec ("ssssss").
  • Repeat 5 times.
  • Hum opening line low in chest. 5 times.

Minutes 3–7: Articulation

  • "Three hundred thirty-three thousand" x3 slow, x3 fast
  • "Worldwide commercial-grade wall" x3
  • "Thirty-three percent word-of-mouth" x3
  • Cork technique: read S1 with cork between teeth, then without

Minutes 7–10: Projection

  • Wall test: say Ask line from 3m, then 5m distance
  • Pitch drop: lower voice on "factory" and "fifteen percent"
  • Target volume: clear at 5 meters without shouting

Minutes 10–15: Full Run

  • Slow-Fast-Slow drill on Section 6
  • Deliver Section 1 at full energy
  • Deliver Section 8 at full energy
  • Drink room-temp water. Rest voice 5 min.

Stumble Recovery Protocol

  1. Stop. Do not power through a mangled word.
  2. Breathe. One breath through the nose.
  3. Restart the sentence. Not the word — the whole sentence. Cleanly.
  4. Keep going. The editor will cut the stumble.

You have 3-5 takes per section. Use them. Nobody delivers a perfect take on the first try, in any language.

Never SaySay Instead

"I think our product is very good" "33% of customers come from word of mouth"
"More or less" / "Let's say" State the exact number. Period.

Production Mistakes

  • Vertical (portrait) video — ABC requires HORIZONTAL
  • Handheld camera — ABC requires TRIPOD
  • Background music — ABC requires NO MUSIC
  • Sitting down — ABC says STAND entire video
  • Reading from visible script — memorize or teleprompter behind camera
  • White shirt, busy patterns, visible BenchK logo on clothing
  • Phone visible, coffee cup on set, watch catching light

Golden Rule

Remember
If you're not 100% sure something is true and verifiable — DON'T say it.
The casting team will fact-check everything. One inflated claim destroys credibility for the entire submission. The strongest pitch is the truthful one.
